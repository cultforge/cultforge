Privacy Policy

Last Updated: May 2026

At CultForge, we believe your data belongs to you. We are an open-source community dedicated to providing the best Minecraft launcher experience, and we are fundamentally opposed to unnecessary data collection, telemetry tracking, and selling user information.

This Privacy Policy explains what small amounts of information we do collect, how it is used, and how third-party services interact with our launcher.

1. Information We Collect

We have designed CultForge to collect the absolute minimum amount of data required to function.

  • Authentication Tokens: To allow you to play Minecraft, our launcher communicates with Microsoft's Xbox Live authentication APIs. We store the resulting OAuth access tokens locally on your machine. We never see, transmit, or store your Microsoft account password.
  • Diagnostic Logs (Opt-in): If the launcher crashes, you may be prompted to share a crash log with our development team. This log includes basic system information (OS version, Java version, allocated RAM) and the stack trace of the error. This is entirely optional and disabled by default.
  • Website Analytics: When you visit cultforge.com, we use privacy-respecting, cookieless analytics to count page views and downloads. This data is anonymized and aggregated.

Zero Telemetry Promise

CultForge does not contain any background telemetry trackers. We do not track what modpacks you play, how long you play, or what servers you connect to. Your gaming habits are exclusively your own business.

2. Third-Party Services

To provide a seamless experience for downloading mods and logging into Minecraft, CultForge must communicate with several third-party APIs. By using these features, you are also subject to their respective privacy policies.

  • Microsoft & Xbox Live: Used for account authentication to verify game ownership. (Microsoft Privacy Statement)
  • Modrinth API: Used when browsing, downloading, or updating mods and modpacks from the Modrinth ecosystem. (Modrinth Privacy Policy)
  • CurseForge API: Used when browsing or downloading content from the CurseForge ecosystem. (Overwolf Privacy Policy)

3. How We Use Information

Any information that is explicitly shared with us (such as crash reports or bug submissions on our GitHub/Discord) is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Fixing bugs and improving the stability of the CultForge launcher.
  • Providing technical support when requested by the user.
  • Preventing malicious use of our automated build servers.

4. Data Storage and Security

Because CultForge operates entirely locally on your machine, your data remains on your hard drive.

Your Microsoft OAuth tokens and local instance configurations are stored in your operating system's standard application data directory (e.g., %APPDATA%/CultForge on Windows). We highly recommend keeping your local machine secure, as anyone with physical or remote access to your file system can access these tokens.

5. Open Source Transparency

Don't just take our word for it. CultForge is 100% open-source software licensed under the hybrid licensing model.

Anyone is free to inspect, audit, and compile the source code from our GitHub Repository to verify exactly what the launcher is doing behind the scenes. We welcome security researchers and community members to review our codebase.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our software or legal requirements. When we do, we will revise the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. If we make significant material changes, we will notify the community via our Discord server and update notes within the launcher.

7. Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please reach out to us:

  • Discord: Open a ticket in the #support channel.
  • GitHub: Open an issue for technical security inquiries.
  • Email: privacy@cultforge.com
